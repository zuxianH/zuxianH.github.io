How to study systematically

This article was last updated: 12 days ago

Here I want to write in detail about my personal systematic learning method. Generally prefer individual self-study.

1. Input and thinking

There are many books in this world, and it is impossible for us to read all of those book. Of course, books can be good or bad. The optimal method of picking out a good book is trying to read it roughly. Rough reading can also be understood as fast reading. The main purpose is to simply look through the table of contents, pictures, and tables in the book, key points and points of interest in the book and to get a general understanding of the content. Sometimes reading personal information about the author can also help you understand the nature of a book better.

In the process of reading, at least for text books, I personally like so-called intensive reading. This is the process of reading a book from thin to thick. This means that every time you read a book, you are not just reading the superficality of the book, but trying to understand and think about the meaning behind each word. Whenever you don’t understand, look up relevant information and leave notes in the book. So slowly you will find that you have read this book thickly.

2. Notes and summary

In terms of notes, I have tried many different solutions. From the paper notes to the electronic notes. From messy methods to Cornell notes. From this, I slowly discovered a system of my own.

At this stage is a process of reading the book from thick to thin. Simply, it is to digest the knowledge you have learned and retell it in the most brief and easy way. Notes are particularly important. I personally use the form of electronic notes, it is a combination of the so-called Ipad + Apple Pencil. In this respect, I think electronic notes have more advantages than traditional notes. First, electronic notes are easier to organize and carry. The second is its versatility and convenience, so there is no need to bring a lot of crayons and so on. Of course, it also has disadvantages. Compared with traditional notes, writing experience and dependence are incomparable.

I personally like to write on notability. In taking notes, I use The Cornell Note-taking System.

3. Emphasis and reflection

When you finish the notes taking, then you can highlight the key points and essence of this book. Whatever and try to ask your self:

  • What is the point of view of this knowledge?
  • What is their basis?
  • How do i apply them to things I already know?
  • What else I might not have thought of, other people or the author have not thought about?

4. Output and improvement

In this regard, I personally think Feynman’s learning method is a very good method. You are in an input state during your learning process. Then the Feynman learning method is an output state. If a person only knows the input but can’t express himself simply what he has learned, I think it is a pity.

“The person who says he knows what he thinks but cannot express it usually does not know what he thinks.” — Mortimer Adler

Richard Feynman, the late Nobel Laureate in physics, was once asked by a Caltech faculty member to explain why spin one-half particles obey Fermi Dirac statistics. Rising to the challenge, he said, “I’ll prepare a freshman lecture on it.” But a few days later he told the faculty member, “You know, I couldn’t do it. I couldn’t reduce it to the freshman level. That means we really don’t understand it.”

As Feynman once said, if a person cannot simply express what he has learned, then he does not understand it at all. Feynman’s learning method is not only an output function, it also has a function that allows you to reflect on what you have learned.

The Feynman learning method is divided into the following four steps:

  1. Be clear about what you want to learn
  2. Use simple language to tell what you have learned.
  3. If you get stuck in the process of telling, review what you have learned
  4. Make your explanation easier to understand, and the language becomes more simplified

The ultimate goal of Feynman’s learning method is to allow you to use your own language to explain what you have learned instead of those hard scientific terms.

About the exercises

I have had this experience

When I was doing the exercises, I couldn’t figure it out when looked at the solution, I could understand everything and then sighed: “I almost thought of this step! Ah, this is so simple, why and how can’t I figure it out? What…”

I guess there is also a bad problem I have now.

I can do the problem but always careless either writing down the wrong question or calculating the wrong number.

How to make myself better:

  1. Do again or reflect on the exercise that you got the wrong answer, it can make you think more rigorous.
    It is inevitable that you will make mistakes when doing the exercises. After reviewing the answer, it is necessary to understand why I did something wrong, and reflect why. If necessary,then record the wrong solution in detail at Error manage book.

    Error Manage Book

  2. Training of proper difficult problems can expand the capacity of thinking.
    Do the exercises as much as possible. Remember, there is no need to do the exercises that you already understand or know, which is a waste of time.

  3. Explain to others, this is an effective way to consolidate your knowledge
    Kage Bunshin no Jutsu

Well, one lives and learns. Finally, I would like to cite Confucius’s famous quote to summarize this section

“学而不思则罔，思而不学则殆。”-孔子
“Learning without thinking leads to confusion;thinking without learning ends in danger.” -Confucius

This sentence comes from Confucius and is also a method of reading and learning that he advocated. This means that if you blindly read books without thinking, you will not be able to use the knowledge of the books reasonably and effectively because you do not deeply understand the meaning of the books, and you will even get confused. If you blindly think about it without actually studying and understand into it. It just like building a tower on the sand and gain nothing at the end.

Pomodoro Technique

根据Pareto principle，这也可以称之为 80/20 rule。

  1. Decide on tasks to be completed
  2. Set the Pomodoro timer to n minutes (usually 25 minutes).
  3. Continue to work until the timer prompts, write down a Pomodoro.
  4. Take a short break for 3-5 minutes.
  5. For every four tomatoes, rest for 15-30 minutes.
