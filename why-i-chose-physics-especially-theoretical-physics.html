Why I chose physics, Especially theoretical physics?

This article was last updated: 9 months ago

First inspiration about physics

The first memory that I actually know about the concept physics is at junior high school. To be honest, in the three year I don’t even know that I had a physics teacher but this did not make me lose my interest in physics.

When I was in second grade at junior high school I readed “The Quantum World” by Kenneth W. Ford. This book makes me think about physics and what is the fundamental of this universe(Sorry Hawking, your book “brief history of time” was not inspiring enough). In this book, I was the first time I really realized how fascinating and beautiful this universe is. In that moment I also realized how ignorable we are to compare the whole universe. The main reason I like physics is it makes me think and be curious all the time. From that day I decided to work with physics like “quantum” stuff or “particle” stuff that even I did not really know what this is. I never was a genius in physics or something. Maybe in my age when I was a child that Albert Einstein already began to think about the universe and I was the little poor boy sitting down in the sandpit and playing in the sand.

I want more challenge and more physics

When I was in the third year of junior high school, I was confused about choosing high school. Most of the people in my class choose a high school near home. In this case, I was the special case, I want to leave home, leave from my parents. I am very lucky and met my SYV or school counselor. She was kind and helped me a lot with how to choose high school. So far I am still very grateful to her. Without her I may not be here, my physics passion may be gone, who knows. Finally, I had a choice for my high school, Polhemskolan at Lund, with orientation gift education in physics. Actually, I had no idea what this meant, but what I knew is my high school would not be bored and I will meet a lot of people with the same passion as me.

That summer I was nervous and waiting for my admission to high school. The day before I could not fall asleep. I woke up early in the morning and updated the admissions website time and time until they uploaded admission notices. At that moment, I am happy and proud of myself. I came in Polhemskolan at Lund.

Polhemskolan, another inspiring place


At Polhemskolan I learned a lot. I had a nice three year there. There I got the elementary knowledge of math and physics. We had study visits e.g. nuclear power at Barsebäck, MAX IV Laboratory, European Spallation Source (and CERN). During the education, we also had the opportunity to take a step at university. We had many lectures from Lund University and we did laboratory work at Lund University. In that way, my perspective on the physical world increased.

It turned out that I was very bad at study physics when I started in high school. I remembered that I stuck to the concept acceleration for several weeks before I fully understood it. It is similar to other concepts like energy conservation and other basic physics concepts. Which is really ridiculous. Sometimes I was so sad and frustrated but I never gave up. No matter how frustrated I was, my physics passion never went away. A quotes from Lao Tzu “ The journey of a thousand miles begins with one step.” gave me a lot of motivation. Sometimes you can not reach the goal directly. You must do it step by step. That makes me enjoy physics in another way.

“People who knows how to study is inferior to people who likes studying; people who likes studying is inferior to people who takes studying as joy.” - Konfucius

It’s interesting for me to think about physics, but the process of learning physics makes me feel more fulfilled.

I think the most important thing I actually learned from polhem was my learning ability. Another advantage of that education was that you can start reading courses at the university level already in high school.

Mathematics is my favorite dessert

从我记事起我觉得我的数学一直都很不错。至少我觉得数学这科目在学校中我觉得是最简单的。虽然我并没有得过什么重大的奖但也是一种喜好吧。

在高二的时候我对于数学的认知有了天翻付地的变化。有时候我觉得我之前我学的数学并不是真正的数学而是‘算数’。在某种意义上来说，我懂的如何去计算一道题，但我几乎好像从来都没刨根问底的去想这背后的意义是什么。我记得那一年我第一次接触到大学数学，微积分。那一年也是我数学的一次考试未及格。

有一次考试对我印象很深刻。那是一门muntan，主要就用用语言去表达这道题的解。那一天我心有成足的去考试，一般大概也就是10分钟。可结果是我和那教授足足坐在那讲了45分钟。从那开始我才认识到我是那么的愚昧与无知。我非常感谢那位教授，是他让我见识到我在数学大厦目前我是多么的弱小。同时也让我看到了数学的不同的角度。

从那以后我就抱了以往不同的心态，以及严谨的态度去学数学，真正意义上的数学。有时候我也曾问过我自己学数学有什么用。其实也没什么用，就像吃甜点那样。爱数学就觉得他好吃，好玩。

I don’t know what theoretical physicist means but it sound fun

说我懂物理其实也不然。懂的也只是一知半解。我了解理论物理学家大多数要不然就是书上或者网上。在现实生活当中我并没有认识过一名真正的理论物理学家。甚至连物理学家到底真正在做什么都是一头雾水。有时候看书的时候觉得那些理论物理学家，比如爱因斯坦，霍金，费曼等人都很酷。所以也就励志的想成为一名理论物理学家。

其实理论物理学家

What I think about real physics v.s. popular physics

It was a interesting sentence that I found on the internet. “Nature magazine’s thickening speed has exceeded the speed of light, but this does not violate the special theory of relativity, because it does not convey any information.” What I think, that makes sense. The more physics I read and know, the more I think that popular science is a fairy tale in the physical world.
